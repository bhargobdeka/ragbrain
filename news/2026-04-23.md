🧠 Learn & Understand

• Agentic RAG (multi-step retrieval): The shift from "retrieve once, generate" to agents that decide when and what to retrieve mid-reasoning. LlamaIndex and Gemini reasoning are leading examples. Matters because it's what separates toy RAG demos from systems that handle real complex queries.
• MCP as infrastructure: Model Context Protocol is solidifying as the standard interface between models, tools, and data — think USB-C for AI. If you haven't dug into how MCP servers work as a first-class primitive, now's the time. The Deep Research update baking it in is a strong signal it's not going away.
• Cognitive density over raw scale: Industry is prioritizing smaller, specialized models for specific agentic tasks (tool-calling, structured reasoning) over monolithic giants. Architecturally important for how you design multi-agent systems and pick models per-node.

🛠️ Build & Practice

• Spin up an MCP server: Pick one data source you care about (a local DB, a private API, Notion, whatever) and expose it via MCP. With Google and Anthropic both leaning into it, fluency here will pay off fast.
• Experiment with OpenAI's Privacy Filter: If any of your pipelines handle user data, drop this into the pre-processing step. It's open-weight and Apache 2.0 — zero reason not to evaluate it this week.
